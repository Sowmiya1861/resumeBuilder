1. Start with a strong resume summary or objective. This is a short statement at the top of your resume that summarizes your experience and presents your most important skills.

2. List your relevant work experience. Include job titles, employers, dates of employment, and a brief description of the duties and accomplishments for each job.

3. Include your educational background. Include the name of the degree, major, school, and year of graduation.

4. Add additional sections. You may want to include volunteer experience, certifications, awards, or other relevant information.

5. Use keywords. Many employers use computer programs to scan resumes for keywords related to the job you are applying for. Make sure to include relevant keywords in your resume.

6. Proofread your resume. It is important to make sure there are no typos or errors in your resume. It is a good idea to have someone else review your resume for accuracy.
